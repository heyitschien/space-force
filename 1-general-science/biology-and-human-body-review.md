# ASVAB Biology & Human Body: High-Yield Study Sheet

Goal: Cover the **minimum complete set** so you can answer both direct facts and “nearby trap” options.

---

## 1) Classification Basics (broad -> specific)

Order to know:

**Kingdom -> Phylum -> Class -> Order -> Family -> Genus -> Species**

- **Definition**: This is the biological hierarchy for classifying organisms.
- **Contrast**:  
  - **Kingdom** = broadest  
  - **Species** = most specific
- **Example**: If asked broadest level in this test format, answer **Kingdom**.

**Memory anchor**:  
- "KPCOFGS"  
- "King = biggest category, Species = most specific."

---

## 2) Feeding Types (must know as a cluster)

- **Carnivore**: eats meat  
- **Herbivore**: eats plants  
- **Omnivore**: eats both

- **Definition**: Feeding type based on diet.
- **Contrast**: Meat-only is **not** omnivore or herbivore.
- **Examples**:  
  - Lion = carnivore  
  - Cow = herbivore  
  - Human = omnivore

**Memory anchor**:  
- "Carn = carne/meat."

---

## 3) Cell Basics

### Core structures
- **Nucleus**: control center (contains DNA)
- **Cell membrane**: boundary; controls what enters/exits
- **Cytoplasm**: gel-like interior where organelles sit
- **Mitochondria**: energy production ("powerhouse")

### Osmosis vs Diffusion
- **Osmosis**: movement of **water** across a membrane
- **Diffusion**: movement of particles from high to low concentration (not limited to water)

- **Definition**: Both are passive transport ideas.
- **Contrast**: Osmosis is a specific type of diffusion (water only).
- **Example**: Water moving into/out of a cell through membrane = osmosis.

**Memory anchors**:
- "Nucleus = command center."
- "Osmosis = water movement."

---

## 4) Body Systems (function + key organs)

- **Digestive**: breaks down food, absorbs nutrients  
  - Organs: stomach, intestines
- **Circulatory**: transports blood, oxygen, nutrients  
  - Organs: heart, blood vessels
- **Respiratory**: gas exchange (O2 in, CO2 out)  
  - Organs: lungs, trachea
- **Nervous**: control and communication  
  - Organs: brain, spinal cord, nerves
- **Skeletal**: support/protection  
  - Organs: bones, joints
- **Muscular**: movement, posture  
  - Organs: skeletal muscles
- **Immune**: defense against pathogens  
  - Components: white blood cells, antibodies

**High-yield fact**:  
- Intestines belong to the **digestive** system.

---

## 5) Blood Basics

- **Red blood cells (RBCs)**: carry oxygen
- **White blood cells (WBCs)**: fight infection; immune defense
- **Platelets**: help blood clot

- **Definition**: Three common blood components with distinct roles.
- **Contrast**:  
  - WBCs do defense, not oxygen transport  
  - RBCs carry oxygen, not antibodies
- **Example**: Infection response = WBC role.

**Memory anchor**:  
- "White blood cells = body defense."

---

## 6) Heart & Lungs Essentials

- **Heart has 4 chambers** (2 atria, 2 ventricles)
- **Lungs** handle oxygen/carbon dioxide exchange

- **Definition**: Heart pumps blood; lungs exchange gases.
- **Contrast**: Heart != gas exchange organ.
- **Example**: O2 enters blood in lungs, then heart circulates that blood.

---

## 7) Joint Types

- **Fixed joints**: no movement (skull)
- **Hinge joints**: one-direction movement (elbow, knee)
- **Ball-and-socket**: wide movement range (shoulder, hip)

- **Definition**: Joint type determines movement.
- **Contrast**: Skull joints are fixed, not hinge.
- **Example**: Immovable skull-type joints = **fixed joints**.

---

## 8) Nutrition Basics (primary role)

- **Protein**: build/repair tissues
- **Carbohydrates**: quick energy
- **Fats**: long-term energy, insulation, cell support
- **Vitamins**: support body processes
- **Minerals**: structure + regulation (e.g., bones, nerve signaling)
- **Water**: transport, temperature control, chemical reactions

**High-yield fact**:  
- Apples are **not** a meaningful protein source.

## Why specific facts alone are not enough

ASVAB often tests direct recall, but answer choices include **nearby truths**.  
So you need:

1. Anchor fact
2. Closest contrast
3. One quick example

Example:
- Anchor: Carnivore = meat-only
- Contrast: Omnivore = both; Herbivore = plants
- Example: Lion = carnivore

---

## Fast Study Method (Definition -> Contrast -> Example)

Use this for every concept:

- **Definition**: what it is
- **Contrast**: what it is not
- **Example**: real-world case

This prevents trap-answer mistakes.
