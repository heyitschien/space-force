## 2) Must-know facts (high yield)

### Earth layers

- **Crust**: outermost layer; where we live; thinnest.
- **Mantle**: middle layer; thickest; mostly solid rock.
- **Core**: innermost layer; very hot; mostly iron.

### Spheres

- **Biosphere**: where life exists (land, water, air where organisms live).
- **Atmosphere**: layer of gases around Earth.
- **Lithosphere**: rigid outer layer (crust + upper mantle).

### Rock types

- **Igneous**: formed from cooled magma/lava (e.g., pumice, granite, basalt).
- **Sedimentary**: formed from compressed sediments (e.g., sandstone, shale, limestone).
- **Metamorphic**: formed from existing rocks under heat/pressure (e.g., marble, slate).

### Biomes (climate + key clue)

- **Tropical rainforest**: high rainfall + high temperatures.
- **Desert**: very low rainfall.
- **Tundra**: very cold; short growing season.
- **Grassland**: moderate rainfall; grasses dominate.
- **Deciduous forest**: moderate seasons; trees lose leaves.

### Crust composition

- Most abundant element in Earth's crust by mass = **oxygen** (~46%).
- Next: silicon (~28%), aluminum (~8%), iron (~5%).

## 3) Definition -> Contrast -> Example map

Use this pattern for every concept:

### Crust

- Definition: Outermost solid layer of Earth.
- Contrast: Not mantle or core.
- Example: Where we live; thinnest layer.

### Biosphere

- Definition: Where life exists on Earth.
- Contrast: Not atmosphere alone (upper atmosphere has no life).
- Example: Oceans, land surface, soil, lower atmosphere.

### Igneous rock

- Definition: Rock formed from cooled magma or lava.
- Contrast: Not sedimentary (compressed sediments) or metamorphic (heat/pressure).
- Example: Pumice, granite, basalt.

### Pumice

- Definition: Light, porous igneous rock from fast-cooling lava with trapped gas bubbles.
- Contrast: Not sedimentary or metamorphic.
- Example: Floats on water; volcanic origin.

### Tropical rainforest

- Definition: Biome with high rainfall and high temperatures year-round.
- Contrast: Not desert (dry) or tundra (cold).
- Example: Amazon, Congo basin.

### Sedimentary rock

- Definition: Rock formed from compressed sediments (sand, mud, shells).
- Contrast: Not igneous or metamorphic.
- Example: Sandstone, shale, limestone.

### Metamorphic rock

- Definition: Rock formed when existing rock is changed by heat and pressure.
- Contrast: Not igneous or sedimentary.
- Example: Marble (from limestone), slate (from shale).

### Oxygen in crust

- Definition: Most abundant element in Earth's crust by mass (~46%).
- Contrast: Not silicon (second most abundant).
- Example: Combines with other elements to form silicates, oxides, carbonates.

## 4) Trap answers you should expect

### Trap 1: layer swap

- "Surface" or "outermost" layer choices may include mantle.
- Fix: crust = outermost; mantle = middle; core = innermost.

### Trap 2: sphere swap

- Biosphere vs atmosphere vs lithosphere can be mixed.
- Fix: biosphere = where life exists; atmosphere = gases; lithosphere = rigid rock layer.

### Trap 3: rock-type swap

- Pumice may appear as sedimentary or metamorphic.
- Fix: pumice = igneous (cooled lava).

### Trap 4: biome swap

- Tropical rainforest vs desert vs grassland by climate clues.
- Fix: tropical rainforest = high rainfall + high temp; desert = low rainfall.

### Trap 5: element swap

- Silicon vs oxygen for "most abundant in crust."
- Fix: oxygen is most abundant by mass.
