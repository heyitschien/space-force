## 2) Must-know facts (high yield)

### Metric conversion

- **1 meter** = 100 centimeters = 1000 millimeters
- **1 kilometer** = 1000 meters
- Common prefixes: kilo (1000×), centi (1/100), milli (1/1000)

### Temperature (Celsius)

- **Boiling point of water**: 100°C
- **Freezing point of water**: 0°C
- **Melting point**: temperature at which solid and liquid exist in equilibrium (or solid becomes liquid)
- **Boiling point**: temperature at which liquid and gas exist in equilibrium

### Speed of light

- **~186,000 miles per second** (not per hour or per minute)
- Radio waves travel at speed of light
- **Light-year**: distance light travels in one year (distance, not time)

### Velocity vs speed

- **Speed**: scalar (magnitude only); "how fast"
- **Velocity**: vector (magnitude + direction); "how fast and in what direction"

### Energy, work, power

- **Energy**: ability to do work; measured in joules (J)
- **Work**: force × distance (W = F × d)
- **Power**: rate of doing work = work/time; measured in **watts** (W)

### Newton's laws

- **1st (Inertia)**: object at rest stays at rest, object in motion stays in motion unless acted on by a force
- **2nd (F = ma)**: force = mass × acceleration; force needed to accelerate is directly proportional to mass
- **3rd (Action-reaction)**: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction

## 3) Definition -> Contrast -> Example map

Use this pattern for every concept:

### 1 meter = 100 centimeters

- Definition: One meter equals 100 centimeters.
- Contrast: Not 10 cm; not 100 mm (1 m = 1000 mm).
- Example: A meter stick has 100 cm marks.

### 1 kilometer = 1000 meters

- Definition: One kilometer equals 1000 meters.
- Contrast: Not 100 m; not 10,000 m.
- Example: A 1 km strip is 1000 m long.

### Celsius boiling point

- Definition: Water boils at 100°C on the Celsius scale.
- Contrast: Not 0°C (freezing); not 212° (Fahrenheit); not 373.15 K.
- Example: At sea level, boiling water = 100°C.

### Celsius freezing point

- Definition: Water freezes at 0°C on the Celsius scale.
- Contrast: Not 100°C (boiling); not 32°F.
- Example: Ice melts at 0°C.

### Speed of light

- Definition: Light travels at approximately 186,000 miles per second.
- Contrast: Per second, not per hour or per minute; not 18,600.
- Example: Radio waves travel at speed of light.

### Light-year

- Definition: Distance light travels in one year.
- Contrast: It is a distance, not a unit of time.
- Example: Proxima Centauri is about 4 light-years away.

### Velocity

- Definition: Vector quantity that measures how fast an object moves and the direction it moves.
- Contrast: Not speed (scalar, no direction); not acceleration.
- Example: "60 mph north" is velocity.

### Speed

- Definition: Scalar quantity; magnitude of motion only.
- Contrast: No direction; not velocity.
- Example: "60 mph" is speed.

### Energy

- Definition: Ability to do work.
- Contrast: Not power (rate of work); not force.
- Example: Stored energy can do work later.

### Power

- Definition: Rate of doing work; work per unit time.
- Contrast: Not energy (ability); not force.
- Example: Measured in watts (W).

### Melting point

- Definition: Temperature at which solid and liquid states exist in equilibrium (solid becomes liquid).
- Contrast: Not boiling point (liquid to gas).
- Example: Ice melts at 0°C.

### Boiling point

- Definition: Temperature at which liquid and gas states exist in equilibrium.
- Contrast: Not melting point (solid to liquid).
- Example: Water boils at 100°C.

### Newton's first law (Inertia)

- Definition: Object at rest stays at rest; object in motion stays in motion unless acted on by a force.
- Contrast: Not 2nd law (F=ma); not 3rd (action-reaction).
- Example: Seatbelt holds you when car stops.

### Newton's second law (F = ma)

- Definition: Force = mass × acceleration; force needed to accelerate is directly proportional to mass.
- Contrast: Not inertia (1st); not action-reaction (3rd).
- Example: Heavier object needs more force to accelerate.

### Newton's third law (Action-reaction)

- Definition: For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.
- Contrast: Not F=ma; not inertia.
- Example: Rocket pushes exhaust; exhaust pushes rocket.

## 4) Trap answers you should expect

### Trap 1: Speed of light unit

- Speed of light may appear as "per hour" or "per minute."
- Fix: ~186,000 miles per **second**.

### Trap 2: Meter conversion

- "10 centimeters" or "100 millimeters" may appear.
- Fix: 1 m = 100 cm; 1 m = 1000 mm (not 100 mm).

### Trap 3: Celsius vs Fahrenheit

- 212° or 0° may appear for boiling.
- Fix: Boiling water = 100°C; freezing = 0°C.

### Trap 4: Velocity vs speed

- "Speed" when direction is mentioned.
- Fix: Velocity = direction + magnitude; speed = magnitude only.

### Trap 5: Energy vs power

- "Power" for ability to do work; "energy" for rate.
- Fix: Energy = ability to do work; power = rate of work (watts).

### Trap 6: Melting vs boiling

- Boiling point when solid-liquid is described.
- Fix: Melting = solid-liquid; boiling = liquid-gas.

### Trap 7: Light-year

- "Light-year" as a unit of time.
- Fix: Light-year = distance, not time.

### Trap 8: Newton law swap

- Inertia when F=ma is described; F=ma when inertia is described.
- Fix: 1st = inertia; 2nd = F=ma; 3rd = action-reaction.
