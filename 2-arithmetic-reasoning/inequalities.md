# 🎯 LEVEL 6 — INEQUALITIES (ASVAB MASTER LESSON)

## 🧠 CORE IDEA (LOCK THIS IN)

An **inequality** means:

* NOT exact
* A **range**
* You are finding **how much is enough / not enough**

### 🔑 Symbols You Must Recognize Instantly

| Symbol | Meaning      | Memory Trick        |
| ------ | ------------ | ------------------- |
| >      | greater than | "more than"         |
| <      | less than    | "less than"         |
| ≥      | at least     | includes the number |
| ≤      | at most      | includes the number |

---

# ⚙️ PRINCIPLE 1 — “MORE THAN” = STRICTLY GREATER

## 💡 Translation Pattern

| Words        | Math |
| ------------ | ---- |
| more than    | >    |
| greater than | >    |
| at least     | ≥    |
| no less than | ≥    |

---

## 📌 VISUAL

```
Profit needed: > 30

|----|----|----|----|----|----|
  0   10   20   30   40

We must go PAST 30 → not equal → NEXT number
```

---

## ✅ EXAMPLE 1

Selling price = $5
Cost = $2
Profit per item = $3

Want: **profit > $30**

```
3x > 30
x > 10
```

👉 Answer: **11 items**

---

## 🧪 PRACTICE

1. Profit per item = $4
   Want more than $20

2. Profit per item = $6
   Want more than $50

---

## ✅ ANSWERS

1. 4x > 20 → x > 5 → **6**
2. 6x > 50 → x > 8.33 → **9**

---

# ⚙️ PRINCIPLE 2 — BREAK-EVEN VS PROFIT

## 💡 KEY EQUATIONS

* Break-even → **Revenue = Cost**
* Profit → **Revenue > Cost**

---

## 📌 VISUAL

```
Revenue line must CROSS cost line

Profit zone →
       /
      /
     /   ← revenue
----/---------
   /
  /
 cost
```

---

## ✅ EXAMPLE 2

Overhead = $1000
Cost per item = $3
Sell = $5

```
Revenue = 5x
Cost = 1000 + 3x

5x > 1000 + 3x
2x > 1000
x > 500
```

👉 Answer: **501 items**

---

## 🧪 PRACTICE

1. Overhead = 200
   Cost = 4
   Sell = 6
   Profit?

2. Overhead = 500
   Cost = 2
   Sell = 5

---

## ✅ ANSWERS

1. 6x > 200 + 4x
   2x > 200 → x > 100 → **101**

2. 5x > 500 + 2x
   3x > 500 → x > 166.67 → **167**

# ⚙️ PRINCIPLE 4 — MINIMIZING COST (INEQUALITY THINKING)

This is **hidden inequality logic**:
👉 “least amount of money”

---

## 💡 METHOD

1. Find **total cost**
2. Compare options
3. Choose smallest

---

## ✅ EXAMPLE 3

Option A: 10 for $50
Option B: 25 for $110

```
A = 5 per unit
B = 4.40 per unit
```

👉 Answer: **B**

---

## ⚠️ ADVANCED (REAL TEST STYLE)

Need 55 gallons:

Option A: 11 gallons for $108
Option B: 6 gallons for $215

---

### Step 1 — Cover requirement

```
A: 55 ÷ 11 = 5 containers → 5 × 108 = 540
B: 55 ÷ 6 ≈ 9.17 → need 10 containers → 10 × 215 = 2150
```

👉 Answer: **Option A**

# 🧠 PATTERN 1 — “MORE THAN” (STRICT INEQUALITY)

## 🧪 Problem

A small business sells handmade bracelets at a weekend market. Each bracelet sells for $12. The cost to make each bracelet is $7. The owner wants to earn **more than $100 in profit** for the day.

What is the **minimum number of bracelets** the owner must sell?

---

## ✅ Solution (Step-by-step thinking)

Profit per bracelet = 12 − 7 = 5

5x > 100
x > 20

👉 Must go past 20 → **21 bracelets**

# 🧠 PATTERN 2 — BREAK-EVEN VS PROFIT

## 🧪 Problem

A food truck owner spends $800 each month on permits and maintenance. Each meal costs $3 to prepare and is sold for $7.

What is the **least number of meals** the owner must sell in a month to **start making a profit**?

---

## ✅ Solution

Revenue = 7x
Cost = 800 + 3x

7x > 800 + 3x
4x > 800
x > 200

👉 **201 meals**

# 🧠 PATTERN 4 — MINIMUM COST (BULK PURCHASE)

## 🧪 Problem

A contractor needs **72 bricks** for a project. He has two purchasing options:

* Option A: Packs of 8 bricks for $24
* Option B: Packs of 15 bricks for $50

Which option will cost **less overall**, and what is the total cost?

---

## ✅ Solution

Option A:
72 ÷ 8 = 9 packs
9 × 24 = **216**

Option B:
72 ÷ 15 = 4.8 → need 5 packs
5 × 50 = **250**

👉 **Option A is cheaper ($216)**

# 🧠 PATTERN 5 — BREAK-EVEN (EQUALITY)

## 🧪 Problem

A company produces custom mugs. The company has fixed costs of $1,200 and each mug costs $4 to make. Each mug sells for $10.

How many mugs must the company sell to **break even**?

---

## ✅ Solution

10x = 1200 + 4x
6x = 1200
x = 200

👉 **200 mugs**

# 🧠 PATTERN 6 — REAL ASVAB STYLE (COMBINED DISTRACTION)

## 🧪 Problem

A school is organizing a fundraiser by selling raffle tickets. Each ticket is sold for $5. The school spent $450 upfront on supplies and printing. In addition, each ticket costs $1 to produce.

The school wants to raise **more than $900 in profit** after covering all costs.

What is the **minimum number of tickets** that must be sold?

---

## ✅ Solution

Revenue = 5x
Cost = 450 + x

Profit condition:

5x > 450 + x + 900
5x > 1350 + x
4x > 1350
x > 337.5

👉 **338 tickets**
