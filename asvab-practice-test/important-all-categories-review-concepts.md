# Important All Categories Review Concepts (V2)

To ace these three specific practice exams, study what they repeatedly test: fast setup of word problems, high-frequency science and electrical facts, tool/engine identification, and visual-spatial pattern recognition.

This v2 guide patches the gaps found in `practice-1.pdf`, `practice-2.pdf`, and `practice-3.pdf`.

## 1) General Science (GS)

Memorize definitions and one-step fact recall.

- **Cell and anatomy:** mitosis vs meiosis vs cleavage, gene/chromosome/DNA, electron/proton basics, spinal cord and brainstem, right ventricle to lungs, red vs white blood cell functions, digestion starts in the mouth.
- **Biology and ecology:** herbivore/carnivore/omnivore, producer vs consumer, chlorophyll and photosynthesis, respiration vs metabolism, common animal examples (for example, kangaroo as marsupial).
- **Earth and space:** crust/mantle/core, atmosphere vs biosphere, moon phases and eclipses, blue moon, planet order and ringed planets, nearest star (Proxima Centauri), major moons (Ganymede/Titan), common rock IDs (igneous example: pumice).
- **Chem/physics facts:** pH (acids < 7, sour taste), boiling and melting points on Celsius scale, velocity as vector quantity, energy vs power, Newton laws (especially inertia and F = m*a concept), speed of light/radio wave behavior.

## 2) Arithmetic Reasoning (AR)

This is mostly translation from words to equations under time pressure.

- **Core equations:** d = r*t, r = d/t, t = d/r; percent = part/whole; interest = P*r*t.
- **Money and pay:** tax, tips, wage raise percent, overtime pay (base plus overtime multiplier), installment total cost.
- **Ratios and fractions:** ratio scaling, fraction of a set, mixed operations with fractions, leftover quantity problems.
- **Unit conversion fluency:** hours-minutes, feet-inches-yards, quarts-gallons, miles-kilometers, dozens and gross.
- **Area and geometry in context:** rectangle area, square yard vs square foot conversion, right-triangle distance (Pythagorean theorem in word problems).
- **Average and target score setup:** required final test score for target average, average monthly cost, weighted totals.
- **Probability basics:** favorable outcomes / total outcomes.
- **Business setup problems:** break-even style logic (fixed cost + variable cost vs selling price), sequential discounts (for example 20% then 15%).

## 3) Word Knowledge (WK)

Expect synonym/antonym and context-meaning questions.

- **High-frequency word bank:** assimilate, proficient, headlong, burnished, hypocrisy, domicile, demise, chasm, susceptible, apex, brittle, erratic, fanatic, fallible, atone, frugal, tacit, licit, supplicate, abrogate, defray, emblem, and related variants.
- **Approach:** use roots/prefixes/suffixes (`auto`, `bene`, `contra`, `-crat`, `-logy`) and sentence context to eliminate distractors.
- **Practice target:** build a rotating deck of 200 to 300 words from missed questions; review daily in mixed order.

## 4) Paragraph Comprehension (PC)

Most misses come from over-inference.

- **Literal detail:** pull exact facts (dates, quantities, names, terms) directly from the passage.
- **Main idea/title:** choose the option broad enough for the whole paragraph, not one detail.
- **Inference:** only infer what is strongly supported; do not import outside knowledge.
- **Author viewpoint:** identify what the author would likely agree with based on tone and claims.

## 5) Mathematics Knowledge (MK)

Pure math execution and concept recall.

- **Algebra:** solve linear equations and inequalities, function substitution, exponent rules (`x^a * x^b = x^(a+b)`), FOIL expansion.
- **Powers and roots:** squares, cubes, fourth roots, square-root estimation and simplification.
- **Number properties:** prime numbers, prime factorization, factorials, mode/mean/median.
- **Geometry facts:** triangle types, angle sums (triangle 180, quadrilateral 360), supplementary/complementary angles, trapezoid basics, line equation from two points.
- **Measurement formulas:** circumference `C = pi*d` (or `2*pi*r`), area formulas, volume `V = l*w*h`.
- **Percent/fraction conversion:** percent to reduced fraction and fraction to percent quickly.
- **Number line distance:** absolute-distance setup including negative values.

## 6) Electronics Information (EI)

Know symbols, units, and one-step calculations.

- **Core laws and units:** Ohm's law `V = I*R` and power `P = V*I`; volts, amps, ohms, watts, hertz.
- **Quick solve variants:** `I = P/V`, `R = V/I`, and unit interpretation under pressure.
- **AC/DC and frequency:** AC vs DC, rectification (AC to DC), household AC standard (60 Hz in North America).
- **Components and ratings:** resistor, capacitor (microfarads), inductor (millihenries), diode (anode/cathode), transformer, transistor types.
- **Circuit behavior:** series vs parallel paths; home wiring is typically parallel.
- **Practical wiring:** wire gauge rule (higher number = smaller diameter), neutral wire color (white/natural), fuse rating safety concept.
- **Instrumentation:** ammeter/current, voltmeter/voltage, ohmmeter/resistance, wattmeter/power.
- **Wave/radio facts:** radio waves travel at speed of light, high-frequency radar context.

## 7) Auto and Shop Information (AS)

Split your prep between engine systems and tool identification.

- **Engine cycle and parts:** intake-compression-power-exhaust, piston rings, connecting rod, crankshaft, camshaft, ignition timing basics.
- **Vehicle systems:** ignition (electronic vs conventional), charging system (alternator and regulator), cooling (water pump and overheating consequences), brake system/master cylinder behavior, limited-slip differential traction logic.
- **Fuel and combustion:** combustion triangle (fuel + air + ignition source), carburetor vs fuel injection, stoichiometric air-fuel concept.
- **Shop tools and usage:** back saw with miter box, rip saw function, box vs open wrench behavior, strap/chain/stillson/torque wrench use cases, countersink purpose, depth gage and calipers.
- **Materials and hardware:** common fasteners and pins, striking vs non-striking tools, lubricants (for example graphite).
- **Torch and cutting basics:** oxy-acetylene pairing for high-temperature cutting.

## 8) Mechanical Comprehension (MC)

Visualize force paths and direction changes quickly.

- **Levers:** class depends on fulcrum/effort/load placement; balance and mechanical advantage setup.
- **Pulleys and tackle:** count supporting rope segments to estimate force advantage.
- **Gears:** meshed gears reverse direction, gear ratio trades speed for torque, identify gear types when shown.
- **Pressure and hydraulics:** `P = F/A`, psi interpretation, Pascal principle applications.
- **Simple machines:** wedge, wheel-and-axle, coupling and clutch concepts.
- **Motion concepts:** inertia law, torque-speed tradeoff (as speed rises, torque generally drops in fixed-power setups).

## 9) Assembling Objects (AO)

No formula shortcut. Score comes from pattern reps.

- **Core skill:** mentally rotate, flip, and align edges/notches; track which sides must touch.
- **Error traps:** mirrored pieces, hidden-edge mismatches, and impossible seam alignment.
- **Training method:** short daily sets, review misses by "why impossible" not just by answer key.

## 10) Final Ace Checklist (Must-Hit)

Before test day, verify you can do all of this quickly:

1. Solve AR setup problems without calculator drift (rate, percent, average, probability, discount chains).
2. Execute MK roots/exponents/geometry-angle problems in under 45 to 60 seconds each.
3. Recall EI units, tools, and law rearrangements instantly.
4. Identify common AS tools/engine parts from wording or diagram cues.
5. Read PC passages without over-inference.
6. Keep WK review active daily with spaced repetition.
7. Do AO visual drills every day for speed and confidence.

If these are all green, this guide now covers the concepts repeatedly seen across your three practice tests.
