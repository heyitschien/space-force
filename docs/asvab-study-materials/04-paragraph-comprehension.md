**Analysis:** The passage emphasizes eliminating time waste and being efficient. The key phrase is "Eliminate the waste of your time."

### Strategy 2: Find Specific Details

### Strategy 3: Make Inferences

**Example Passage:**
> Cloud seeding is accomplished by dropping particles of dry ice (solid carbon dioxide) from a plane onto super-cooled clouds. This process encourages condensation of water droplets in the clouds, which usually, but not always, results in rain or snow.

**Question:**
> From this passage, it's reasonable to assume that
> - **(A) cloud seeding could be used to end a drought** âœ“
> - (B) cloud seeding is prohibitively expensive
> - (C) cloud seeding is rarely used
> - (D) cloud seeding can be accomplished by using regular ice

**Analysis:** If cloud seeding produces rain/snow, it logically could help end droughts. The passage doesn't mention cost or frequency, and specifically says "dry ice" not regular ice.

## ğŸ¯ Problem-Solving Strategies

<div style="background: #fff9c4; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px; margin: 20px 0; border-left: 5px solid #fbc02d;">
  <h3 style="color: #f57f17; margin-top: 0;">ğŸ’¡ Reading Comprehension Tips</h3>
  <ol style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Read the passage first</strong> - Get the full picture before answering</li>
    <li><strong>Read questions carefully</strong> - Note if asking for main idea, detail, or inference</li>
    <li><strong>Eliminate wrong answers</strong> - Cross out obviously incorrect choices</li>
    <li><strong>Find evidence</strong> - Locate the part of passage that supports your answer</li>
    <li><strong>Watch for "not" or "except"</strong> - These reverse the question</li>
    <li><strong>Don't overthink</strong> - Answer based on what passage says, not outside knowledge</li>
  </ol>
