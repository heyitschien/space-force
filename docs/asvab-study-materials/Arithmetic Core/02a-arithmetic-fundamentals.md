### Addition: Combining Quantities

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 What Addition Means</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Addition</strong> = Combining two or more quantities to find the total</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think: "How many in all?" or "What's the total?"</p>
</div>

**Visual Example:**
```
3 apples + 4 apples = 7 apples total
```

**Key Words:** sum, total, combined, together, plus, add

---

#### Example 1: Simple Addition

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Sarah has 23 books. She buys 17 more books. How many books does she have in total?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify what you're adding
- Sarah's current books: 23
- New books: 17
- We want the total

**Step 2:** Set up the addition
```
  23
+ 17
----
```

**Step 3:** Add the ones place (right column)
- 3 + 7 = 10
- Write 0, carry 1 to tens place

**Step 4:** Add the tens place (left column)
- 2 + 1 + 1 (carried) = 4
- Write 4

**Step 5:** Write the answer
```
  23
+ 17
----
  40
```

**Answer:** 40 books

**Why This Works:** Addition combines quantities. We add the ones place first, then the tens place, carrying when needed.

---

#### Example 2: Addition with Carrying

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">A store sold $156 worth of merchandise in the morning and $287 in the afternoon. What was the total sales for the day?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the numbers to add
- Morning sales: $156
- Afternoon sales: $287
- Total: ?

**Step 2:** Align numbers by place value
```
  156
+ 287
-----
```

**Step 3:** Add ones place
- 6 + 7 = 13
- Write 3, carry 1

**Step 4:** Add tens place
- 5 + 8 + 1 (carried) = 14
- Write 4, carry 1

**Step 5:** Add hundreds place
- 1 + 2 + 1 (carried) = 4
- Write 4

**Step 6:** Final answer
```
  156
+ 287
-----
  443
```

**Answer:** $443

**Why This Works:** When a column adds up to 10 or more, we "carry" the extra to the next column, just like counting past 9.

---

#### Example 3: Adding Multiple Numbers

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Jenny's test scores are 93, 89, 96, and 98. What is the sum of all her test scores?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** List all numbers to add
- 93 + 89 + 96 + 98

**Step 2:** Add two at a time (easier mentally)
- First pair: 93 + 89 = 182
- Second pair: 96 + 98 = 194

**Step 3:** Add the results
- 182 + 194 = 376

**OR add step-by-step:**
```
  93
  89
  96
+ 98
-----
```

**Step 4:** Add column by column
- Ones: 3 + 9 + 6 + 8 = 26 → Write 6, carry 2
- Tens: 9 + 8 + 9 + 9 + 2 (carried) = 37 → Write 7, carry 3
- Hundreds: 3 (carried) = 3

**Answer:** 376

**Why This Works:** You can add numbers in any order. Grouping makes it easier to manage carrying.

### Subtraction: Taking Away or Finding Difference

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 What Subtraction Means</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Subtraction</strong> = Taking away from a quantity OR finding the difference between two quantities</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think: "How many are left?" or "How much more/less?"</p>
</div>

**Visual Example:**
```
10 cookies - 3 cookies = 7 cookies left
OR
10 cookies - 3 cookies = 7 cookies difference
```

**Key Words:** difference, less, minus, subtract, take away, remaining

---

#### Example 1: Simple Subtraction

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Miriam bought 5 cases of motor oil. A case normally costs $24.00, but she paid $22.50 per case. How much did she save per case?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify what you're finding
- Regular price: $24.00
- Sale price: $22.50
- We want the difference (savings)

**Step 2:** Set up subtraction
```
  24.00
- 22.50
-------
```

**Step 3:** Subtract hundredths place
- 0 - 0 = 0

**Step 4:** Subtract tenths place
- 0 - 5 → Can't do directly, need to borrow
- Borrow from ones: 4 becomes 3, 0 becomes 10
- 10 - 5 = 5

**Step 5:** Subtract ones place
- 3 - 2 = 1

**Step 6:** Subtract tens place
- 2 - 2 = 0

**Answer:** $1.50 saved per case

**Why This Works:** When you can't subtract in a column, borrow from the next higher place value.

---

#### Example 2: Subtraction with Borrowing

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">A security guard walks 6 city blocks. If he walks at a pace of 8 blocks every 30 minutes, how long does it take to walk one block?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Understand the problem
- Total blocks: 6
- Time for 8 blocks: 30 minutes
- We need time per block

**Step 2:** This is actually division (30 ÷ 8), but let's think of it as subtraction
- How many times can we subtract 8 from 30?
- Actually, this is better solved with division (see Division section)

**Alternative approach - Finding difference:**
- If 8 blocks take 30 minutes
- Time per block = 30 ÷ 8 = 3.75 minutes

**Step 3:** Using subtraction to verify:
- 30 - 8 = 22 (1 block walked, 22 min left)
- 22 - 8 = 14 (2 blocks, 14 min left)
- 14 - 8 = 6 (3 blocks, 6 min left)
- 6 < 8, so we have 3 full blocks + 6/8 = 3.75 minutes per block

**Answer:** 3.75 minutes per block

**Why This Works:** Subtraction can help us understand division by seeing how many times we can subtract.

---

#### Example 3: Finding Difference

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">

- Original space: 42 sq ft per person
- New space: 36 sq ft per person
- Find the difference

**Step 2:** Set up subtraction
```
  42
- 36
----
```

**Step 3:** Subtract ones place
- 2 - 6 → Can't do, borrow from tens
- 4 becomes 3, 2 becomes 12
- 12 - 6 = 6

**Step 4:** Subtract tens place
- 3 - 3 = 0

**Answer:** 6 square feet less per employee

**Why This Works:** Finding the difference tells us how much one quantity is more or less than another.

### Multiplication: Repeated Addition

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 What Multiplication Means</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Multiplication</strong> = Adding the same number multiple times</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think: "How many groups of X?" or "X times Y"</p>
</div>

**Visual Example:**
```
3 × 4 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12
(3 groups of 4)
```

**Key Words:** times, multiplied by, product, groups of, per (when combined with quantity)

**When to Multiply:**
- Finding total when you have "X per Y"
- Finding area (length × width)
- Finding total cost (price × quantity)
- Finding distance (speed × time)

---

#### Example 1: Basic Multiplication

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">If a car is towed 12 miles and the charge is $3.50 per mile, how much does the tow cost?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify what you're multiplying
- Rate: $3.50 per mile
- Quantity: 12 miles
- Formula: Cost = Rate × Quantity

**Step 2:** Set up multiplication
```
  3.50
×   12
------
```

**Step 3:** Multiply by ones place (2)
- 2 × 0 = 0 (hundredths)
- 2 × 5 = 10 → Write 0, carry 1 (tenths)
- 2 × 3 + 1 = 7 (ones)
- Result: 7.00

**Step 4:** Multiply by tens place (1, which is actually 10)
- 1 × 0 = 0 (hundredths)
- 1 × 5 = 5 (tenths)
- 1 × 3 = 3 (ones)
- Result: 35.0 (shifted one place left because it's tens)

**Step 5:** Add the results
```
   7.00
+ 35.00
------
  42.00
```

**Answer:** $42.00

**Why This Works:** "Per mile" means multiply. Each mile costs $3.50, so 12 miles = 12 × $3.50.

---

#### Example 2: Multiplication with Carrying

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">How many square feet are needed to carpet a 12-foot × 12-foot room?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the formula
- Area = Length × Width
- Length: 12 feet
- Width: 12 feet

**Step 2:** Set up multiplication
```
  12
× 12
----
```

**Step 3:** Multiply by ones place (2)
- 2 × 2 = 4 (ones)
- 2 × 1 = 2 (tens)
- Result: 24

**Step 4:** Multiply by tens place (1, which is 10)
- 1 × 2 = 2 (tens)
- 1 × 1 = 1 (hundreds)
- Result: 120 (shifted left)

**Step 5:** Add results
```
   24
+ 120
-----
  144
```

**Answer:** 144 square feet

**Why This Works:** Area measures how many square units fit in a space. 12 × 12 = 144 square feet.

#### Example 3: Word Problem - "Per" Means Multiply

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">A waitress earns an average tip of 12% of the cost of food. If she serves $375 worth of food, how much in tips does she earn?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the "per" relationship
- Rate: 12% per dollar of food
- Quantity: $375
- Tips = 12% × $375

**Step 2:** Convert percentage to decimal
- 12% = 12 ÷ 100 = 0.12

**Step 3:** Multiply
```
  375
× 0.12
------
```

**Step 4:** Multiply by hundredths (2)
- 2 × 5 = 10 → Write 0, carry 1
- 2 × 7 + 1 = 15 → Write 5, carry 1
- 2 × 3 + 1 = 7
- Result: 750 (but this is actually 7.50 because we're multiplying by 0.12)

**Step 5:** Multiply by tenths (1)
- 1 × 5 = 5
- 1 × 7 = 7
- 1 × 3 = 3
- Result: 37.5 (shifted one place)

**Step 6:** Add (or use shortcut: 375 × 0.12)
- 375 × 0.12 = 45.00

**Answer:** $45.00

**Why This Works:** "12% of" means multiply. 12% of $375 = 0.12 × 375 = 45.

### Division: Splitting or Grouping

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 What Division Means</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Division</strong> = Splitting into equal groups OR finding how many times one number fits into another</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think: "How many in each group?" or "How many groups?"</p>
</div>

**Visual Example:**
```
12 ÷ 3 = 4
(12 split into 3 groups = 4 in each group)
OR
(How many groups of 3 fit into 12? = 4 groups)
```

**Key Words:** divided by, split, share equally, per (when finding rate), quotient

**When to Divide:**
- Finding average (total ÷ count)
- Finding rate (total ÷ time)
- Finding unit price (total cost ÷ quantity)
- Converting units (larger to smaller)

---

#### Example 1: Basic Division

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">A sales manager buys antacid in bottles by the gross. If he uses 3 bottles per day, how long will 144 bottles (1 gross) last?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify what you're dividing
- Total bottles: 144 (1 gross)
- Usage rate: 3 bottles per day
- Find: How many days?

**Step 2:** Set up division
- Days = Total ÷ Rate per day
- Days = 144 ÷ 3

**Step 3:** Divide using long division
```
   48
3 ) 144
   -12
   ---
    24
   -24
   ---
     0
```

**Step 4:** Check your work
- 48 days × 3 bottles/day = 144 bottles ✓

**Answer:** 48 days

**Why This Works:** Division splits the total into equal groups. 144 bottles ÷ 3 per day = 48 days.

---

#### Example 2: Finding Average (Division)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">

</div>

```
  120.37
  108.45
+ 114.86
--------
  343.68
```

**Step 3:** Divide sum by count
- Average = $343.68 ÷ 3

**Step 4:** Long division
```
  114.56
3 ) 343.68
   -3
   ---
    04
   - 3
   ---
    13
   -12
   ---
     16
    -15
    ---
      18
     -18
     ---
       0
```

**Answer:** $114.56 average per month

**Why This Works:** Average divides the total equally among all items. Total spending ÷ number of months = average per month.

#### Example 3: Finding Rate (Division)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>

</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the formula
- Distance = Speed × Time
- Rearrange: Time = Distance ÷ Speed

**Step 2:** Plug in values
- Time = 1,650 miles ÷ 50 mph

**Step 3:** Divide
```
    33
50 ) 1650
   -150
   ----
    150
   -150
```

**Step 4:** Verify

### Order of Operations: PEMDAS

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 Critical Rule: PEMDAS</h3>
  <p style="font-size: 1.2em; margin: 10px 0;"><strong>P</strong>arentheses → <strong>E</strong>xponents → <strong>M</strong>ultiplication/<strong>D</strong>ivision → <strong>A</strong>ddition/<strong>S</strong>ubtraction</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Multiplication and Division are equal - do left to right</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Addition and Subtraction are equal - do left to right</p>
</div>

**Example:**
```
8 + 3 × 2 = ?
Wrong: 8 + 3 × 2 = 11 × 2 = 22 ❌
Right: 8 + 3 × 2 = 8 + 6 = 14 ✓
(Multiplication before addition!)
```

**Step-by-step:**
1. **P**arentheses: (8 + 3) × 2 = 11 × 2 = 22
2. **E**xponents: 2³ + 4 = 8 + 4 = 12
3. **M**ultiplication/**D**ivision (left to right): 8 ÷ 2 × 4 = 4 × 4 = 16
4. **A**ddition/**S**ubtraction (left to right): 10 - 3 + 2 = 7 + 2 = 9

**Memory Tip:** "Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally"

### Finding Common Denominators: Step-by-Step

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 Why We Need Common Denominators</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">You can only add or subtract fractions when they have the <strong>same denominator</strong></p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think: You can't add apples and oranges unless you convert them to "fruit"!</p>
</div>

**Method 1: List Multiples (Easiest)**

#### Example 1: Simple Fractions (¼ + ⅓)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Find the common denominator for ¼ and ⅓</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** List multiples of each denominator
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**, 16, 20, 24...
- Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, **12**, 15, 18...

**Step 2:** Find the smallest number in both lists
- **LCD = 12** (Least Common Denominator)

**Step 3:** Convert ¼ to have denominator 12
- Ask: "What times 4 equals 12?" → 3
- Multiply numerator and denominator by 3: ¼ × ³⁄₃ = ³⁄₁₂
- Check: 1 × 3 = 3, 4 × 3 = 12 ✓

**Step 4:** Convert ⅓ to have denominator 12
- Ask: "What times 3 equals 12?" → 4
- Multiply numerator and denominator by 4: ⅓ × ⁴⁄₄ = ⁴⁄₁₂
- Check: 1 × 4 = 4, 3 × 4 = 12 ✓

**Step 5:** Now both fractions have the same denominator
- ¼ = ³⁄₁₂
- ⅓ = ⁴⁄₁₂
- **Now you can add:** ³⁄₁₂ + ⁴⁄₁₂ = ⁷⁄₁₂

**Why This Works:** Multiplying by ³⁄₃ or ⁴⁄₄ is the same as multiplying by 1, so the value doesn't change. We're just cutting the pieces into smaller, equal-sized parts.

---

#### Example 2: More Complex (⅙ + ⁸⁄₁₅)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Find the common denominator for ⅙ and ⁸⁄₁₅</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** List multiples (this might take longer, so use prime factorization instead)
- Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24, **30**, 36...
- Multiples of 15: 15, **30**, 45, 60...
- LCD = 30

**Step 2:** Convert ⅙ to have denominator 30
- Ask: "What times 6 equals 30?" → 5
- Multiply: ⅙ × ⁵⁄₅ = ⁵⁄₃₀

**Step 3:** Convert ⁸⁄₁₅ to have denominator 30
- Ask: "What times 15 equals 30?" → 2
- Multiply: ⁸⁄₁₅ × ²⁄₂ = ¹⁶⁄₃₀

**Step 4:** Both fractions now have denominator 30
- ⅙ = ⁵⁄₃₀
- ⁸⁄₁₅ = ¹⁶⁄₃₀
- **Now you can add:** ⁵⁄₃₀ + ¹⁶⁄₃₀ = ²¹⁄₃₀ = ⁷⁄₁₀ (simplified)

**Answer:** LCD = 30

**Why This Works:** Even with larger numbers, the process is the same: find what number both denominators divide into evenly.

#### Example 3: Three Fractions (¼ + ⅓ + ⅙)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Find the common denominator for ¼, ⅓, and ⅙</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** List multiples of all three denominators
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**, 16, 20...
- Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, **12**, 15...
- Multiples of 6: 6, **12**, 18, 24...
- **LCD = 12** (first number in all three lists)

**Step 2:** Convert ¼ to 12ths
- ¼ × ³⁄₃ = ³⁄₁₂

**Step 3:** Convert ⅓ to 12ths
- ⅓ × ⁴⁄₄ = ⁴⁄₁₂

**Step 4:** Convert ⅙ to 12ths
- Ask: "What times 6 equals 12?" → 2
- ⅙ × ²⁄₂ = ²⁄₁₂

**Step 5:** All three fractions now have denominator 12
- ¼ = ³⁄₁₂
- ⅓ = ⁴⁄₁₂
- ⅙ = ²⁄₁₂
- **Now you can add:** ³⁄₁₂ + ⁴⁄₁₂ + ²⁄₁₂ = ⁹⁄₁₂ = ¾ (simplified)

**Answer:** LCD = 12

**Why This Works:** When you have multiple fractions, find the LCD that works for ALL denominators. The LCD must be divisible by each denominator.

### Adding Fractions: Why Common Denominators Matter

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 The Rule</h3>
  <p style="font-size: 1.2em; margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Add numerators, keep denominator the same</strong></p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">But ONLY after finding common denominator!</p>
</div>

**Why Common Denominators?**
```
You can't add: ½ + ⅓ directly
Because: "half" and "third" are different sized pieces!

Visual:
[████] [    ]  +  [██] [██] [  ] = ???
 ½       ½         ⅓   ⅓   ⅓

Convert to same size pieces:
[████] [████] [    ]  +  [████] [████] [████] = [████] [████] [████] [████] [    ]
 ³⁄₆      ³⁄₆     ³⁄₆      ²⁄₆      ²⁄₆      ²⁄₆        ⁵⁄₆
```

#### Example 1: Basic Addition (½ + ¼)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Add ½ + ¼</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Check if denominators are the same
- ½ has denominator 2
- ¼ has denominator 4
- They're different, so we need a common denominator

**Step 2:** Find LCD
- Multiples of 2: 2, **4**, 6...
- Multiples of 4: **4**, 8...
- LCD = 4

**Step 3:** Convert ½ to have denominator 4
- ½ × ²⁄₂ = ²⁄₄

**Step 4:** Convert ¼ (already has denominator 4)
- ¼ = ¼ (no change needed)

**Step 5:** Add numerators, keep denominator
- ²⁄₄ + ¼ = ³⁄₄

**Answer:** ¾

**Why This Works:** Once fractions have the same denominator, you're adding pieces of the same size. Just add how many pieces you have total.

---

#### Example 2: Different Denominators (⅔ + ¾)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Add ⅔ + ¾</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Find LCD
- Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, **12**...
- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, **12**...
- LCD = 12

**Step 2:** Convert ⅔ to 12ths
- Ask: "What times 3 equals 12?" → 4
- ⅔ × ⁴⁄₄ = ⁸⁄₁₂

**Step 3:** Convert ¾ to 12ths
- Ask: "What times 4 equals 12?" → 3
- ¾ × ³⁄₃ = ⁹⁄₁₂

**Step 4:** Add numerators
- 8 + 9 = 17

**Step 5:** Keep denominator
- ¹⁷⁄₁₂

**Step 6:** Simplify (if needed)
- ¹⁷⁄₁₂ = 1 ⁵⁄₁₂ (mixed number)

**Answer:** 1 ⁵⁄₁₂ or ¹⁷⁄₁₂

**Why This Works:** Both fractions are now in "twelfths" - same size pieces. Adding 8 twelfths + 9 twelfths = 17 twelfths.

#### Example 3: Mixed Numbers

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">

- ⁵⁄₂ × ³⁄₃ = ¹⁵⁄₆

**Step 4:** Convert ⁴⁄₃ to 6ths
- ⁴⁄₃ × ²⁄₂ = ⁸⁄₆

**Step 6:** Simplify to mixed number

**Why This Works:** Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first, then add normally. Convert back to mixed number at the end.

### Multiplying Fractions: Why Multiply Top and Bottom

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 The Rule</h3>

**Why It Works:**
```
½ × ⅓ = ?

Think: "Half of a third"
Visual: Take ⅓, then take half of it = ⅙

Formula: (1 × 1) / (2 × 3) = 1/6 ✓
```

- ¹⁄₆

- ¹⁄₆ is already in simplest form

### Dividing Fractions: Why Flip and Multiply

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 The Rule</h3>
  <p style="font-size: 1.2em; margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Flip the second fraction, then multiply</strong></p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">"Keep, Change, Flip"</p>
</div>

**Why Flip?**
```
½ ÷ ⅓ = ?

Think: "How many thirds fit into a half?"
Actually easier: "How many times does ⅓ go into ½?"

Better way: ½ ÷ ⅓ = ½ × ³⁄₁ = ³⁄₂ = 1½
(Flip ⅓ to ³⁄₁, then multiply)
```

#### Example 1: Basic Division (½ ÷ ⅓)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Divide ½ ÷ ⅓</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Keep the first fraction
- ½ stays as ½

**Step 2:** Change ÷ to ×
- ½ ÷ ⅓ becomes ½ × ?

**Step 3:** Flip the second fraction (find reciprocal)
- ⅓ flipped = ³⁄₁ (swap numerator and denominator)

**Step 4:** Multiply
- ½ × ³⁄₁ = ³⁄₂

**Step 5:** Simplify or convert to mixed number
- ³⁄₂ = 1½

**Answer:** 1½

**Why This Works:** Dividing by ⅓ is the same as multiplying by 3. "How many thirds fit into a half?" Answer: 1.5 thirds.

---

#### Example 2: Complex Division (2⅔ ÷ 1¼)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Divide 2⅔ ÷ 1¼</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions
- 2⅔ = ⁸⁄₃ (2 × 3 + 2 = 8)
- 1¼ = ⁵⁄₄ (1 × 4 + 1 = 5)

**Step 2:** Keep, Change, Flip
- Keep: ⁸⁄₃
- Change: ÷ becomes ×
- Flip: ⁵⁄₄ becomes ⁴⁄₅

**Step 3:** Multiply
- ⁸⁄₃ × ⁴⁄₅ = ³²⁄₁₅

**Step 4:** Simplify
- 32 and 15 share no common factors

**Step 5:** Convert to mixed number
- 32 ÷ 15 = 2 remainder 2
- ³²⁄₁₅ = 2 ²⁄₁₅

**Answer:** 2 ²⁄₁₅

**Why This Works:** Always convert mixed numbers first, then use "Keep, Change, Flip" to divide.

### Converting Fractions to Decimals

**Simple Fractions:**

#### Example 1: Simple Conversion (½ = ?)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">

-   0.

- Write 5
- 2 ) 1.0
-   0.5
-    10
-   -10

### Converting Decimals to Fractions

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 Method: Read the Decimal Place</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Write as fraction, then simplify</p>
</div>

#### Example 1: Terminating Decimal (0.75 = ?)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 0.75 to a fraction</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the decimal place
- 0.75 has 2 decimal places (hundredths place)

**Step 2:** Read it as a fraction
- "Seventy-five hundredths" = ⁷⁵⁄₁₀₀

**Step 3:** Write as fraction
- 0.75 = ⁷⁵⁄₁₀₀

**Step 4:** Simplify by finding GCF
- Factors of 75: 1, 3, 5, 15, 25, 75
- Factors of 100: 1, 2, 4, 5, 10, 20, 25, 50, 100
- GCF = 25

**Step 5:** Divide numerator and denominator by GCF
- 75 ÷ 25 = 3
- 100 ÷ 25 = 4
- ⁷⁵⁄₁₀₀ = ³⁄₄

**Answer:** ¾

**Why This Works:** The decimal place tells you the denominator. Two decimal places = hundredths (100).

---

#### Example 2: Complex Decimal (0.375 = ?)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 0.375 to a fraction</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Count decimal places
- 0.375 has 3 decimal places (thousandths place)

**Step 2:** Write as fraction
- 0.375 = ³⁷⁵⁄₁₀₀₀

**Step 3:** Simplify
- Find GCF of 375 and 1000
- 375 = 3 × 5³ = 3 × 125
- 1000 = 2³ × 5³ = 8 × 125
- GCF = 125

**Step 4:** Divide by GCF
- 375 ÷ 125 = 3
- 1000 ÷ 125 = 8
- ³⁷⁵⁄₁₀₀₀ = ³⁄₈

**Answer:** ⅜

**Why This Works:** Three decimal places = thousandths (1000). Always simplify to lowest terms.

**Step 1:** Count decimal places

### Why "Of" Means Multiply

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 The Key Concept</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">When you see "X% of Y", it means "X% times Y"</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">"Of" = multiplication!</p>
</div>

**Why?**
```
"50% of 200" means "50 out of every 100 of 200"

Think: Half of 200 = 100
Formula: 50% × 200 = 0.50 × 200 = 100 ✓
```

**Examples:**
- "12% of $375" = 0.12 × $375 = $45
- "25% of 80" = 0.25 × 80 = 20
- "10% of $8.50" = 0.10 × $8.50 = $0.85

**Memory Tip:** "Of" = "times" (multiplication)

## 4️⃣ Unit Conversions: The Systematic Approach <a id="unit-conversions"></a>

### Understanding Conversion Factors

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">

### Unit Cancellation Method (Dimensional Analysis)

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 The Foolproof Method</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Set up conversion factors so units cancel, leaving desired unit</p>
</div>

#### Example 1: Single-Step Conversion (3 feet to inches)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 3 feet to inches</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify conversion factor
- 1 foot = 12 inches
- Conversion factor: 12 inches / 1 foot = 1

**Step 2:** Set up conversion
- 3 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot)

**Step 3:** Cancel units
- Feet cancel out, leaving inches

**Step 4:** Multiply
- 3 × 12 = 36

**Answer:** 36 inches

**Why This Works:** Larger unit (feet) to smaller unit (inches) = multiply. Each foot has 12 inches.

---

#### Example 2: Area Conversion (288 ft² to yd²)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 288 square feet to square yards</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Understand area conversion
- 1 yard = 3 feet
- 1 square yard = 3 ft × 3 ft = 9 square feet
- Conversion factor: 1 yd² / 9 ft² = 1

**Step 2:** Set up conversion (smaller to larger = divide)
- 288 ft² ÷ 9 ft²/yd²

**Step 3:** Divide
- 288 ÷ 9 = 32

**Answer:** 32 square yards

**Why This Works:** Smaller unit (ft²) to larger unit (yd²) = divide. Square yards are bigger than square feet.

---

#### Example 3: Multi-Step Conversion (3 feet, 8 inches to total inches)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 3 feet, 8 inches to total inches</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Start with what you have
- 3 feet, 8 inches

**Step 2:** Convert feet to inches
- 3 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 36 inches
- (feet cancel out!)

**Step 3:** Add remaining inches
- 36 inches + 8 inches = 44 inches

**Answer:** 44 inches

**Why This Works:** Convert the mixed units to a single unit, then add.

---

#### Example 4: Multi-Step Chain (3 tons to ounces)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert 3 tons to ounces</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Plan the conversion chain
- Tons → Pounds → Ounces

**Step 2:** Convert tons to pounds
- 1 ton = 2,000 pounds
- 3 tons × (2,000 lbs / 1 ton) = 6,000 lbs

**Step 3:** Convert pounds to ounces
- 1 pound = 16 ounces
- 6,000 lbs × (16 oz / 1 lb) = 96,000 oz

**Step 4:** Or do it all at once
- 3 tons × (2,000 lbs / 1 ton) × (16 oz / 1 lb)
- = 3 × 2,000 × 16 oz
- = 96,000 oz

**Answer:** 96,000 ounces

**Why This Works:** Chain conversions step-by-step, canceling units as you go. Each step multiplies by a conversion factor equal to 1.

### Multi-Step Conversions

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Chain Conversions</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Convert step-by-step, canceling units as you go</p>
</div>

**Example:** Convert 3 tons to ounces

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Tons to pounds:** 3 tons × (2,000 lbs / 1 ton) = 6,000 lbs
2. **Pounds to ounces:** 6,000 lbs × (16 oz / 1 lb) = 96,000 oz

**Visual Chain:**
```
3 tons → [× 2000] → 6,000 lbs → [× 16] → 96,000 oz
```

**All at Once:**
```
3 tons × (2000 lbs / 1 ton) × (16 oz / 1 lb)
= 3 × 2000 × 16 oz
= 96,000 oz
```

## 5️⃣ Ratios & Proportions <a id="ratios-proportions"></a>

### Understanding Ratios

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 What Ratios Represent</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">A ratio compares two quantities</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Can be written as: 3:4 or ³⁄₄ or "3 to 4"</p>
</div>

**Examples:**
- "3 apples for every 4 oranges" = 3:4
- "Speed of 60 miles per hour" = 60:1 or 60/1
- "2 parts water, 3 parts juice" = 2:3

### Setting Up Proportions

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 Proportion = Two Equal Ratios</h3>
  <p style="font-size: 1.1em; margin: 10px 0;"><strong>a/b = c/d</strong> (read: "a is to b as c is to d")</p>
</div>

#### Example 1: Simple Proportion (3 apples cost $2, 9 apples cost ?)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">If 3 apples cost $2, how much do 9 apples cost?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Set up the proportion
- Known ratio: 3 apples / $2
- Unknown ratio: 9 apples / $x
- ³⁄₂ = ⁹⁄ₓ (keep units consistent!)

**Step 2:** Cross-multiply
- 3 × x = 2 × 9
- 3x = 18

**Step 3:** Solve for x
- x = 18 ÷ 3
- x = 6

**Answer:** $6

**Why This Works:** The ratio of apples to cost stays constant. If 3 apples cost $2, then 9 apples (3 times more) cost $6 (3 times more).

---

#### Example 2: Rate Problems (60 miles in 2 hours, ? miles in 5 hours)

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">If you travel 60 miles in 2 hours, how many miles will you travel in 5 hours at the same speed?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Set up proportion
- Known: 60 miles / 2 hours
- Unknown: x miles / 5 hours
- ⁶⁰⁄₂ = ˣ⁄₅

**Step 2:** Cross-multiply
- 60 × 5 = 2 × x
- 300 = 2x

**Step 3:** Solve
- x = 300 ÷ 2
- x = 150

**Answer:** 150 miles

**Why This Works:** Speed (rate) stays constant. Distance is proportional to time.

---

#### Example 3: Scale Problems

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">On a map, 1 inch represents 25 miles. If two cities are 3.5 inches apart on the map, how far apart are they in reality?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Set up proportion
- Map scale: 1 inch / 25 miles
- Map distance: 3.5 inches / x miles
- ¹⁄₂₅ = ³.⁵⁄ₓ

**Step 2:** Cross-multiply
- 1 × x = 25 × 3.5
- x = 87.5

**Answer:** 87.5 miles

**Why This Works:** The scale ratio stays constant. Map distance is proportional to real distance.

### Cross-Multiplication: Why It Works

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 The Method</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">If a/b = c/d, then a × d = b × c</p>
</div>

**Why It Works:**
```
If a/b = c/d, multiply both sides by b × d:

(a/b) × (b × d) = (c/d) × (b × d)
a × d = c × b

So: a × d = b × c ✓
```

**Example:** Solve ²⁄₃ = ˣ⁄₁₂

1. **Cross-multiply:** 2 × 12 = 3 × x
2. **Simplify:** 24 = 3x
3. **Solve:** x = 8
4. **Check:** ²⁄₃ = ⁸⁄₁₂ ✓ (both = 0.667)

  <p style="font-size: 1.2em; margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Rate = Distance ÷ Time</strong></p>
</div>

**The Three Formulas:**
```
Distance = Rate × Time
Rate = Distance ÷ Time
Time = Distance ÷ Rate
```

**Memory Tip:** "DIRT" - Distance Is Rate Times Time

**Example:** If you travel 60 miles in 2 hours, what's your speed?

**Solution:**
- Rate = Distance ÷ Time
- Rate = 60 miles ÷ 2 hours = 30 miles per hour

### "Per" Problems: Multiply or Divide?

<h4 style="margin-top: 0; color: #e65100; font-size: 1.1em;">🟠 Conversions</h4>
<ul style="margin: 0; font-size: 0.9em; padding-left: 20px;">
<li>Larger → Smaller: Multiply</li>
<li>Smaller → Larger: Divide</li>
<li>Use unit cancellation</li>
<li>Chain conversions step-by-step</li>
</ul>

<ul style="margin: 0; font-size: 0.9em; padding-left: 20px;">
<li>"Per" + quantity → Multiply</li>
