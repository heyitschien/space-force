### 📌 Formula:

[
V = l \times w \times h
]

Where:

* l = length
* w = width
* h = height

Think:
👉 “Volume is stacking area upward.”
Area of base = l × w
Then multiply by height.

---

### ✅ Example 1

A box is:

* Length = 6 ft
* Width = 4 ft
* Height = 3 ft

[
V = 6 × 4 × 3
]
[
V = 72 \text{ cubic feet}
]

---

### ⚠ ASVAB Trap

If they give:

* inches
* feet
* yards

Everything must match units.

Volume is ALWAYS in:

* cubic inches
* cubic feet
* cubic meters

NOT square.

![Image](https://d138zd1ktt9iqe.cloudfront.net/media/seo_landing_files/base-area-of-cylinder-1630302261.png)

### 📌 Formula:

[
V = \pi r^2 h
]

Where:

* r = radius (NOT diameter)
* h = height
* π ≈ 3.14 (unless told otherwise)

---

### Why This Formula?

Base of a cylinder = circle
Area of circle = πr²

Then multiply by height.

Same logic as box:
Base area × height

---

### ✅ Example 2

Radius = 3 inches
Height = 5 inches

[
V = 3.14 × 3^2 × 5
]
[
V = 3.14 × 9 × 5
]
[
V = 3.14 × 45
]
[
V = 141.3 \text{ cubic inches}
]

---

# 🔥 ASVAB Common Traps

### 1️⃣ They give diameter instead of radius

If diameter = 10
Radius = 5

Always divide by 2.

---

### 2️⃣ Forgetting to square the radius

r² means:
[
r × r
]

Not ×2.

---

### 3️⃣ Unit confusion

If radius is in feet
Height in inches

Convert first.

---

# 🧠 Memory Hacks (Military Fast Recall Style)

Box:
👉 “LWH”

Cylinder:
👉 “Pie R squared H”

Say it like a rhythm:
PIE – R – SQUARED – H

---

# ⚡ Quick Comparison

| Shape    | Formula |
| -------- | ------- |
| Box      | lwh     |
| Cylinder | πr²h    |

Both are:
👉 Base area × height
