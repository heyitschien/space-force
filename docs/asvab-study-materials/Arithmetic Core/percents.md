# The Percent Master Lesson (ASVAB Ready)

## 1. The Core Intuition

A **percent** simply means:

> **parts out of 100**

So:

| Percent | Meaning       |
| ------- | ------------- |
| 50%     | 50 out of 100 |
| 25%     | 25 out of 100 |
| 10%     | 10 out of 100 |
| 1%      | 1 out of 100  |

Think of a **100-square grid**.

# 2. The Universal Percent Formula

Every percent problem is actually this:

\text{Part} = \text{Whole} \times \frac{\text{Percent}}{100}

Meaning:

**portion = total × percent**

Example:

15% of 80

= 80 × 0.15
= **12**

# 3. The 3 Types of Percent Problems (ASVAB)

Almost every ASVAB percent question is **one of these three**.

| Type | Question                        |
| ---- | ------------------------------- |
| 1    | Percent **of** a number         |
| 2    | What **percent** is A of B      |
| 3    | **Increase / decrease** percent |

Master these and **90% of ASVAB percent questions are solved**.

---

# TYPE 1

# Percent of a Number

### Principle

\text{Part} = \text{Whole} \times \frac{\text{Percent}}{100}

---

### Example 1

15% of 80

Convert percent to decimal.

15% = **0.15**

80 × 0.15 = **12**

Answer: **12**

---

### Example 2

20% of 375

0.20 × 375

= **75**

---

### Fast Mental Trick

Break into **10% chunks**

Example:

15% of 80

10% = 8
5% = 4

Total = **12**

This is how test-takers solve it **in 5 seconds**.

---

# TYPE 2

# What Percent Is A of B?

Example:

18 out of 24 students passed.

What percent passed?

Principle:

\text{Percent} = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100

Compute:

18 ÷ 24 = 0.75

0.75 × 100 = **75%**

Answer: **75%**

---

### Mental Shortcut

Convert fraction.

18/24 simplifies to

3/4

You should memorize these:

| Fraction | Percent |
| -------- | ------- |
| 1/2      | 50%     |
| 1/3      | 33%     |
| 2/3      | 67%     |
| 1/4      | 25%     |
| 3/4      | 75%     |
| 1/5      | 20%     |

These appear **constantly on ASVAB**.

---

# TYPE 3

# Percent Increase / Decrease

Example:

A shirt costs $40.
25% off.

Principle:

\text{New Price} = \text{Original} \times (1 - \frac{\text{Percent}}{100})

Compute:

25% = 0.25

40 × 0.75

= **30**

Answer: **$30**

---

# Successive Discounts (Common ASVAB Trap)

Example:

$300 item
20% off
then 15% off

Most people do:

35% off ❌ WRONG

Correct method:

300 × 0.80 × 0.85

= 204

Final price **$204**

# ASVAB Style Example

### Question

A waitress earns 12% tips.

She serves $375 worth of food.

How much tips?

---

Step 1

10% of 375 = 37.5

Step 2

2% of 375 = 7.5

Add:

37.5 + 7.5 = **45**

Answer: **$45**

Guests invited: 120
Guests arrived: 90

How many percent **did NOT attend?**

Step 1

Missing = 30

Step 2

30 / 120 = 0.25

= **25%**

Answer: **25%**

# The 10-Second Mental Math Tricks

These are **gold for ASVAB speed**.

| Percent | Shortcut                |
| ------- | ----------------------- |
| 10%     | move decimal left       |
| 1%      | move decimal two places |
| 5%      | half of 10%             |
| 25%     | divide by 4             |
| 50%     | divide by 2             |
| 20%     | divide by 5             |

Example:

25% of 240

240 ÷ 4 = **60**

Done instantly.

# Harder ASVAB Problems

### Problem 1

A car price increases from $18,000 to $21,600.

Percent increase?

Old = 18,000
Increase = 3,600

3,600 / 18,000

= 0.20

= **20%**

---

### Problem 2

A jacket costs $80.
Tax is 8%.

Total price?

8% of 80

10% = 8
2% = 1.6

8 − 1.6 = **6.4**

Total:

80 + 6.4

= **86.40**

# The Most Common ASVAB Percent Traps

### Trap 1

Percent **of what number?**

Always identify the **base**.

---

### Trap 2

Successive discounts

Never add percentages.

---

### Trap 3

Percent increase vs percent difference.

Always divide by **original number**.
