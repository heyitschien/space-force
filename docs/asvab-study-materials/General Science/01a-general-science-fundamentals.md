## 1️⃣ Astronomy Fundamentals

### Understanding Our Solar System

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 The Solar System</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Our solar system consists of the Sun and all objects that orbit it, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Concept:</strong> The Sun is at the center, and planets orbit around it due to gravity.</p>
</div>

### Planet Order (From Sun Outward)

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 Memory Tip: "My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos"</h3>
  <ol style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Mercury</strong> - Closest to Sun, smallest planet</li>
    <li><strong>Venus</strong> - Hottest planet, brightest in sky</li>
    <li><strong>Earth</strong> - Our home planet</li>
    <li><strong>Mars</strong> - Red planet, thin CO₂ atmosphere</li>
    <li><strong>Jupiter</strong> - Largest planet, gas giant</li>
    <li><strong>Saturn</strong> - Famous for rings, gas giant</li>
    <li><strong>Uranus</strong> - Ice giant, rotates sideways</li>
    <li><strong>Neptune</strong> - Farthest planet, ice giant</li>
  </ol>
</div>

### Key Planet Facts

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 Essential Facts</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Largest planet:</strong> Jupiter</li>
    <li><strong>Smallest planet:</strong> Mercury</li>
    <li><strong>Brightest planet (after sun/moon):</strong> Venus</li>
    <li><strong>Planets with rings:</strong> Saturn (most visible), Jupiter, Uranus, Neptune (4 total)</li>
    <li><strong>Hottest planet:</strong> Venus (thick atmosphere traps heat)</li>
    <li><strong>Red planet:</strong> Mars (iron oxide gives red color)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### The Asteroid Belt

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 Location</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Asteroid belt:</strong> Located between Mars and Jupiter</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Contains thousands of rocky objects (asteroids) that orbit the Sun.</p>
</div>

### Stars and Distances

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 Light-Year Concept</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Light-year:</strong> The distance light travels in one year</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Speed of light:</strong> 186,000 miles per second</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Closest star (after Sun):</strong> Proxima Centauri (about 4.2 light-years away)</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Why use light-years:</strong> Stars are so far away that regular distance units are impractical</p>
</div>

### Planetary Atmospheres

<div style="background: #e1f5fe; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #0277bd; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #0277bd; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Atmosphere Composition</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Earth:</strong> Mostly nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%)</li>
    <li><strong>Mars:</strong> Mostly carbon dioxide (CO₂)</li>
    <li><strong>Venus:</strong> Mostly carbon dioxide (CO₂)</li>
    <li><strong>Jupiter/Saturn:</strong> Mostly hydrogen and helium (gas giants)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## 2️⃣ Cell Biology Fundamentals

### Understanding Cells: The Basic Unit of Life

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 What is a Cell?</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Cell:</strong> The basic unit of life. All living organisms are made of cells.</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Think of cells as tiny factories that carry out all life processes.</p>
</div>

### Cell Structure and Organelles

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Key Cell Components</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Nucleus:</strong> Control center of the cell - contains DNA</li>
    <li><strong>Mitochondria:</strong> Powerhouse - produces energy (ATP)</li>
    <li><strong>Ribosomes:</strong> Protein factories - make proteins</li>
    <li><strong>Cell Membrane:</strong> Outer boundary - controls what enters/exits</li>
    <li><strong>Cytoplasm:</strong> Gel-like substance filling the cell</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### Cell Types

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Prokaryotic:</strong> Simple cells without a nucleus (bacteria)</li>
    <li><strong>Eukaryotic:</strong> Complex cells with a nucleus (plants, animals, fungi)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## 3️⃣ Human Body Systems Fundamentals

### Understanding Body Systems

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 Body Systems Overview</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">The human body consists of multiple systems that work together to maintain life. Each system has specific organs and functions.</p>
</div>

### Circulatory System

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Function: Transport</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Circulates blood throughout the body</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Organs:</strong></p>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Heart:</strong> Pumps blood (4 chambers: 2 atria + 2 ventricles)</li>
    <li><strong>Blood Vessels:</strong> Arteries (carry blood away), Veins (carry blood back)</li>
    <li><strong>Blood:</strong> Red blood cells (carry oxygen), White blood cells (fight infection)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### Digestive System

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 Function: Break Down Food</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Processes food and absorbs nutrients</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Organs:</strong> Mouth, Esophagus, Stomach, Intestines (small and large), Liver</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Process:</strong> Food → Digestion → Absorption → Elimination</p>
</div>

### Respiratory System

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 Function: Gas Exchange</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Brings oxygen in, removes carbon dioxide</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Organs:</strong> Lungs, Trachea (windpipe), Bronchi</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Process:</strong> Breathe in oxygen → Exchange in lungs → Breathe out CO₂</p>
</div>

### Nervous System

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 Function: Control & Communication</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Controls body functions and processes information</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Organs:</strong> Brain, Spinal Cord, Nerves</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Process:</strong> Receives signals → Processes → Sends responses</p>
</div>

### Skeletal System

<div style="background: #e1f5fe; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #0277bd; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #0277bd; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Function: Support & Protection</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Provides structure and protects organs</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Components:</strong> Bones, Joints, Cartilage</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Joint Types:</strong> Fixed (skull), Hinge (knee, elbow), Ball-and-socket (shoulder, hip), Pivot (neck)</p>
</div>

### Immune System

<div style="background: #ffebee; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #f44336; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 Function: Defense</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Purpose:</strong> Fights infections and diseases</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Key Components:</strong></p>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>White Blood Cells:</strong> Fight infections and produce antibodies</li>
    <li><strong>Antibodies:</strong> Proteins that attack foreign invaders</li>
  </ul>
</div>

#### Example 1: Heart Structure

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h4 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">Problem</h4>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">The human heart includes how many chambers?</p>
</div>

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

**Step 1:** Recall heart structure
- The heart is a pump with multiple chambers

**Step 2:** Identify the number
- Human heart has 4 chambers
- 2 upper chambers (atria) + 2 lower chambers (ventricles)

**Answer:** 4 chambers

**Why This Works:** Mammals have 4-chambered hearts for efficient blood circulation. Two chambers pump blood to the lungs, two pump blood to the body.

## 4️⃣ Classification & Taxonomy Fundamentals

### Understanding Biological Classification

<div style="background: #f3e5f5; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #9c27b0; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #7b1fa2; margin-top: 0;">🟣 Why Classify?</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Scientists classify living things to organize and understand the diversity of life on Earth.</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Classification helps us see relationships between organisms.</p>
</div>

### The Classification Hierarchy

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 From Broadest to Most Specific</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Memory Tip:</strong> "King Philip Came Over For Good Soup"</p>
  <ol style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Kingdom</strong> ← Broadest level (e.g., Animalia, Plantae)</li>
    <li><strong>Phylum</strong> (e.g., Chordata - animals with backbones)</li>
    <li><strong>Class</strong> (e.g., Mammalia - mammals)</li>
    <li><strong>Order</strong> (e.g., Carnivora - carnivores)</li>
    <li><strong>Family</strong> (e.g., Felidae - cats)</li>
    <li><strong>Genus</strong> (e.g., Felis - small cats)</li>
    <li><strong>Species</strong> ← Most specific (e.g., Felis catus - domestic cat)</li>
  </ol>
</div>

### The Five Kingdoms System

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 Five Major Kingdoms</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Animalia:</strong> Animals (multicellular, move, consume food)</li>
    <li><strong>Plantae:</strong> Plants (multicellular, make their own food via photosynthesis)</li>
    <li><strong>Fungi:</strong> Mushrooms, molds (decomposers)</li>
    <li><strong>Protista:</strong> Simple organisms (algae, protozoa)</li>
    <li><strong>Monera:</strong> Bacteria (single-celled, no nucleus)</li>
  </ul>
</div>

### Domain vs. Kingdom (Practice-Test Add‑On)

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 Modern Classification: Domains</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Domain</strong> is a classification level <strong>above Kingdom</strong> in many modern systems.</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">The 3 domains are:</p>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8; margin-top: 10px;">
    <li><strong>Bacteria</strong> (prokaryotes)</li>
    <li><strong>Archaea</strong> (prokaryotes, different from bacteria)</li>
    <li><strong>Eukarya</strong> (organisms with a nucleus: animals, plants, fungi, protists)</li>
  </ul>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>High‑ROI test note:</strong> Older “Monera” (5‑kingdom model) roughly corresponds to what modern systems split into <strong>Bacteria</strong> + <strong>Archaea</strong>.</p>
</div>

  <h3 style="color: #c62828; margin-top: 0;">🔴 What is an Atom?</h3>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Atom:</strong> The basic unit of matter. Everything is made of atoms.</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">Atoms consist of a nucleus (center) and electrons (orbiting around).</p>

### Subatomic Particles

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">

  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Protons:</strong> Positively charged, in nucleus</li>
    <li><strong>Neutrons:</strong> No charge (neutral), in nucleus</li>
    <li><strong>Electrons:</strong> Negatively charged, orbit nucleus</li>

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">

  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Atomic Number:</strong> The number of protons in an atom's nucleus</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;">This number determines what element the atom is!</p>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0;"><strong>Example:</strong> Carbon has 6 protons, so its atomic number is 6</p>
</div>

- Electrons have a negative charge

## 1️⃣1️⃣ States of Matter Fundamentals

### Understanding Matter States

<div style="background: #e8f5e9; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #4caf50; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #2e7d32; margin-top: 0;">🟢 Three Common States</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Solid:</strong> Fixed shape and volume, particles tightly packed</li>
    <li><strong>Liquid:</strong> Fixed volume, takes shape of container, particles can move</li>
    <li><strong>Gas:</strong> No fixed shape or volume, particles move freely</li>
  </ul>

### Phase Transitions

<div style="background: #e3f2fd; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #2196f3; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #1976d2; margin-top: 0;">🔵 Changing States</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Melting:</strong> Solid → Liquid (at melting point)</li>
    <li><strong>Freezing:</strong> Liquid → Solid (at freezing point)</li>
    <li><strong>Boiling:</strong> Liquid → Gas (at boiling point)</li>
    <li><strong>Condensation:</strong> Gas → Liquid</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div style="background: #fff3e0; padding: 15px; border-radius: 8px; border-left: 5px solid #ff9800; margin: 15px 0;">
  <h3 style="color: #e65100; margin-top: 0;">🟠 The Fast Distinction</h3>
  <ul style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Scalar:</strong> magnitude only (example: <strong>speed</strong> = 30 mph)</li>
    <li><strong>Vector:</strong> magnitude + direction (example: <strong>velocity</strong> = 30 mph east)</li>
  </ul>
  <p style="margin: 10px 0 0 0;"><strong>High-ROI:</strong> If a question mentions “direction,” it’s pointing you toward a <strong>vector</strong>.</p>

    <li><strong>Mantle:</strong> Middle layer, thickest, mostly solid rock</li>
    <li><strong>Core:</strong> Inner layer, very hot, mostly iron</li>
  </ul>

    <li><strong>Sedimentary:</strong> Formed from compressed sediments</li>

  <ol style="line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Evaporation:</strong> Water turns to vapor (liquid → gas)</li>

    <li><strong>Collection:</strong> Water collects in oceans, lakes, rivers</li>
  </ol>
