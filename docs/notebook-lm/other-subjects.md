Based on the practice tests provided, here are the fundamental principles and main ideas you must master to excel in the remaining ASVAB categories. 

**1. General Science (GS)**
This section tests your high school-level knowledge of life, earth, and physical sciences. 
*   **Biology & Life Science:** Understand cell division (e.g., cell divisions forming an embryo is called cleavage). Know the functions of the body, such as white blood cells carrying antibodies to fight infection and digestion starting in the mouth. Understand basic plant biology, such as chlorophyll being the green coloring that allows for photosynthesis. Know ecological terms like biomes (e.g., tropical rainforests are characterized by high temperatures and lots of rainfall).
*   **Physics:** Know Newton's laws, specifically the law of inertia, which states the force needed to accelerate an object is directly proportional to its mass. Understand the difference between speed and velocity (velocity is a vector quantity that measures how fast an object moves *and* its direction).
*   **Earth & Space Science:** Memorize the order of the planets (Mars is fourth from the sun) and basic astronomy (e.g., Proxima Centauri is the closest star to Earth, excluding the sun). Know the types of rocks (igneous rocks like pumice are formed from cooling molten material) and earth structure (the surface is called the crust). 
*   **Chemistry:** Know basic temperature scales (e.g., the boiling point of water on the Celsius scale is 100°).

**2. Electronics Information (EI)**
You must know the principles of electricity, circuits, and electrical components.
*   **Ohm’s Law:** This is the most crucial formula: **Voltage = Current × Resistance** ($V = I \times R$ or $E = I \times R$).
*   **Electrical Units:** Memorize what units measure what properties. Power is measured in watts. Current is measured in amperes (amps). Resistance is measured in ohms. Frequency (cycles per second) is measured in Hertz (Hz).
*   **Current Types:** Know the difference between Alternating Current (AC) and Direct Current (DC). The typical house runs on AC, which is delivered in North America at 60 Hz. A rectifier is used to change AC to DC.
*   **Components & Symbols:** 
    *   *Capacitors:* Store electricity/electrons.
    *   *Diodes:* Contain an anode and cathode. A bridge rectifier contains 4 diodes.
    *   *Transistors:* Act as active elements in a circuit. Common types are NPN and PNP.
    *   *Resistors:* Learn how to read color bands (e.g., Brown, Black, Red indicates a 1,000-ohm resistor because brown=1, black=0, and red=2 additional zeros).
*   **Circuits:** Know that an electrical circuit with only one path for current is a **series circuit**. The most common type of circuit in a house is a **parallel circuit**.

**3. Auto & Shop Information (AS)**
This section requires practical knowledge of automotive systems, engine repair, and shop tools.
*   **The 4-Stroke Engine Cycle:** Memorize the four strokes in order: Intake, Compression, Power, and Exhaust. Ignition and firing occur during the Power stroke.
*   **Engine Components:** 
    *   *Piston Rings:* Seal the combustion chamber and allow the pistons to move freely. 
    *   *Connecting Rod:* Connects the crankshaft to the piston.
    *   *Water Pump:* Moves coolant through the cooling system to control heat.
    *   *Master Cylinder:* A primary component of the automotive braking system.
    *   *Alternator:* Converts an automobile's mechanical energy into electrical energy.
*   **Tools & Uses:** Know specific tools for specific jobs.
    *   *Saws:* A ripsaw cuts with the grain of the wood. A hacksaw cuts metal. A back saw is used with a miter box.
    *   *Measuring/Leveling:* A plumb bob checks for vertical trueness, while a level checks for horizontal trueness. Calipers (inside/outside) are used to measure the thickness of wire or curves.
    *   *Wrenches:* A box wrench is advantageous over an open-ended wrench because it is less likely to slip.

**4. Mechanical Comprehension (MC)**
You must be able to calculate forces, understand simple machines, and evaluate mechanical drawings.
*   **Levers:** Levers consist of a fulcrum, effort, and resistance. Learn to calculate the **Mechanical Advantage (MA)** of a first-class lever: **$MA = \text{Length of Effort Arm} \div \text{Length of Resistance Arm}$**. Moving the fulcrum changes the effort needed to move a load.
*   **Gears:** Meshing gears (teeth together) turn in **opposite directions**. To find how many revolutions a smaller gear makes compared to a larger one, divide the number of teeth on the larger gear by the number of teeth on the smaller gear.
*   **Pressure & Fluids:** Understand the formula **Pressure = Force $\div$ Area** ($P = F \div A$). If evaluating boxes with the same volume, the box with the largest bottom area will have the shortest sides and exert pressure differently.
*   **Thermodynamics:** Metals (like a steel key or silver tool) are better conductors than wood or plastic. Therefore, metal objects will feel colder on a cold day and heat up much faster in boiling water.

**5. Word Knowledge (WK) & Paragraph Comprehension (PC)**
*   **Word Knowledge:** This is a pure vocabulary test determining synonyms or antonyms. You must memorize definitions or use root words/prefixes/suffixes to deduce meanings. (e.g., "Commend" means to express approval of, "Erratic" means likely to perform unpredictably, "Assimilate" means to absorb). 
*   **Paragraph Comprehension:** This tests your reading comprehension. The key principle here is to read the short passage carefully and strictly separate the **main idea** from **supporting details**. Never use outside knowledge; base your answer *only* on what is explicitly stated or logically inferred from the text provided.

**6. Assembling Objects (AO)**
*   This section tests spatial reasoning. You will be given a diagram of disassembled 2D shapes with connection points (dots and lines) and must mentally visualize how they fit together to form a final shape. The principle here is to pay close attention to the angles of the shapes and the exact placement of the dots indicating where the pieces connect.
