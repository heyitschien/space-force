Algebra & Coordinate Geometry

Slope of a Line

Change in $y$ over change in $x$:

$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

Quadratic Formula

For $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$

Equations of Lines

  • Standard: $Ax + By = C$
  • Slope-Intercept: $y = mx + b$
  • Point-Slope: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

Distance & Midpoint

Distance:

$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$

Midpoint:

$M = (\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2})$

2D Geometry & Polygons

Triangle Sum Theorem

Sum of interior angles = $180^\circ$

Pythagorean Theorem

$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$

Area of a Triangle

$A = \frac{1}{2}bh$

Circles

Area: $A = \pi r^2$

Circumference: $C = 2\pi r = \pi d$

Polygon Interior Sum

$S = 180(n - 2)$

Trapezoid Area

$A = \frac{1}{2}(b_1 + b_2)h$

Volume & Surface Area

Shape Volume ($V$) Surface Area ($SA$)
Cube $a^3$ $6a^2$
Rect. Solid $l \times w \times h$ $2(lw + wh + hl)$
Cylinder $\pi r^2 h$ $2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2$
Sphere $\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ $4\pi r^2$
Pyramid $\frac{1}{3}abh$ Sum of bases & faces
Cone $\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$ $\pi r^2 + \pi rl$

Laws of Exponents & Radicals

Exponents Rules

  • Zero Power: $a^0 = 1$
  • Product Rule: $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$
  • Quotient Rule: $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$
  • Power of Power: $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$
  • Negative Rule: $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$

Radical Properties

  • Fractional Exp: $a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a}$
  • General: $a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m$
  • Product Prop: $\sqrt[n]{ab} = \sqrt[n]{a} \cdot \sqrt[n]{b}$
  • Quotient Prop: $\sqrt[n]{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt[n]{a}}{\sqrt[n]{b}}$

Factoring Guide

STEP 1

Always check for a Greatest Common Factor (GCF) first!

2 Terms

Difference of Squares:
$A^2 - B^2 = (A-B)(A+B)$

Sum of Cubes:
$A^3 + B^3 = (A+B)(A^2 - AB + B^2)$

3 Terms

Trinomials ($x^2 + bx + c$):
Find numbers that multiply to $c$ and add to $b$.

Perfect Squares:
$(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$

4 Terms

Factor by Grouping!

Group terms 2-by-2 and find the common binomial.

Units & Conversions (ASVAB Quick Reference)

How to convert (fast + reliable)

  • Step 1: Write the value with units (e.g., $3 \text{ ft}$).
  • Step 2: Multiply by a conversion fraction that cancels units (e.g., $\frac{12\text{ in}}{1\text{ ft}}$).
  • Step 3: If converting area or volume, square/cube the factor: 1 ft = 12 in → $1 \text{ ft}^2 = 144 \text{ in}^2$, $1 \text{ ft}^3 = 1728 \text{ in}^3$.

Length (US Customary)

  • 12 in = 1 ft
  • 3 ft = 1 yd
  • 1760 yd = 1 mi
  • 5280 ft = 1 mi

Tip: If the word problem mixes ft/in, convert first before computing area/perimeter.

Length (Metric)

  • 10 mm = 1 cm
  • 100 cm = 1 m
  • 1000 m = 1 km

Metric prefixes: kilo (1000), centi (1/100), milli (1/1000).

Mass / Weight

  • 16 oz = 1 lb
  • 2000 lb = 1 ton
  • 1000 g = 1 kg

ASVAB usually uses these simple relationships—memorize them.

Capacity (US)

  • 3 tsp = 1 tbsp
  • 2 tbsp = 1 fl oz
  • 8 fl oz = 1 cup
  • 2 cups = 1 pt
  • 2 pt = 1 qt
  • 4 qt = 1 gal

Capacity (Metric)

  • 1000 mL = 1 L

If a problem mixes L and mL, convert to one before computing.

Time

  • 60 sec = 1 min
  • 60 min = 1 hr
  • 24 hr = 1 day

Used constantly with $d = rt$ word problems.
