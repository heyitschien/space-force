# 🧠 LEVEL 2 MASTER LESSON: RATIOS & PROPORTIONS

## 🔥 PART 1 — CORE PRINCIPLE (INTUITION FIRST)

### 💡 What is a Ratio?

A **ratio = relationship between two quantities**

👉 Think:

> “For every ___, there are ___”

Examples:

* 3 trucks → 24 houses
* 3:4 red to blue balls
* 1/2 inch → 1 mile

---

### 💡 What is a Proportion?

A **proportion = two equal ratios**

👉 Think:

> “This situation scales the same way”

Example:
[
\frac{3}{24} = \frac{x}{72}
]

---

## 🧠 THE GOLDEN RULE (MOST IMPORTANT)

👉 **Ratios are scaling machines**

If one side changes → the other side scales the same way.

---

## 🔥 PART 2 — 3 CORE ASVAB PATTERNS

You will see ONLY these 3 patterns (disguised):

---

### 🔹 TYPE 1: DIRECT PROPORTION (most common)

👉 More → More
👉 Less → Less

Example from your test:

> 3 trucks → 24 houses
> ? trucks → 72 houses 

---

### 🔹 TYPE 2: UNIT RATE (hidden ratio)

👉 “per 1” thinking

Example:

* $3.50 per mile
* 12 oz per meal
* 8 blocks per 30 minutes

---

### 🔹 TYPE 3: SCALE FACTOR

👉 How many times bigger?

Example:

* 24 → 72 = ×3
* So trucks also ×3

---

# ⚔️ PART 3 — WORKED EXAMPLES (STEP-BY-STEP)

---

## 🟢 Example 1 (Direct Ratio)

> 3 trucks → 24 houses
> ? trucks → 72 houses

### Step 1: Find scale

[
72 ÷ 24 = 3
]

### Step 2: Apply scale

[
3 × 3 = 9
]

✅ Answer: **9 trucks**

---

## 🟢 Example 2 (Ratio Split)

> Ratio 3:4 (red:blue), 80 blue balls

### Step 1: Understand meaning

For every 4 blue → 3 red

### Step 2: Scale

[
80 ÷ 4 = 20
]

### Step 3: Apply

[
3 × 20 = 60
]

✅ Answer: **60 red balls**

---

## 🟢 Example 3 (Map Scale)

> 1/2 inch → 1 mile
> 4.5 inches → ?

### Step 1: Convert

[
4.5 ÷ 0.5 = 9
]

### Step 2: Apply

[
9 miles
]

✅ Answer: **9 miles**

---

# 🧠 PART 4 — MENTAL MODELS (ELITE UNDERSTANDING)

### 🧩 MODEL 1: “PER ONE” (BEST TOOL)

Always ask:

> “What is ONE unit worth?”

---

### 🧩 MODEL 2: “SCALE FACTOR”

Ask:

> “How many times bigger?”

---

### 🧩 MODEL 3: “CROSS MULTIPLY (BACKUP)”

If stuck:
[
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}
\Rightarrow ad = bc
]

# 🏋️ PART 5 — DRILLS (BUILD SPEED)

---

## 🔹 SIMPLE DRILLS (build instinct)

1. 2 pencils cost $6 → 1 pencil = ?
2. 5 workers → 20 jobs → 1 worker = ?
3. 10 miles in 2 hours → speed?

---

## 🔹 MEDIUM DRILLS (ASVAB style)

1. 4 machines make 80 parts → how many for 200?
2. 6 gallons lasts 3 days → how long will 20 gallons last?
3. 12 oz → 3 meals → 60 oz → ?

---

## 🔹 HARD DRILLS (multi-step)

1. A truck carries 2 tons feeding 50 people for 5 days → how many people for 10 days?

2. 8 workers build a fence in 6 days → how many workers for 3 days?

---

## 🔥 ELITE DRILLS (ASVAB HARD MODE)

These are EXACTLY like the hardest problems you saw (FEMA, mixtures, etc.)

---

### ⚔️ ELITE 1 (FEMA STYLE)

> Each person needs:

* Breakfast = 12 oz
* Lunch = 18 oz
* Dinner = 18 oz

Truck = 3 tons for 10 days

👉 How many people?

---

### ⚔️ ELITE 2 (MIXED RATIO + UNIT)

> A car travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours.
> How far in 5 hours?

---

### ⚔️ ELITE 3 (MULTI-LAYER)

> 3 pipes fill a tank in 6 hours
> How long for 6 pipes?

---

# 🧠 PART 6 — TEST STRATEGY (THIS IS KEY)

From your actual practice exams:

👉 Most AR questions are:

* disguised ratios
* multi-step scaling
* unit conversions + ratios combined 

---

## ⚡ FAST METHOD (WHAT YOU SHOULD DO)

1. Identify ratio
2. Find scale factor
3. Apply once
4. DONE

---

## 🚨 COMMON TRAPS

❌ Mixing units (feet vs yards)
❌ Not scaling BOTH sides
❌ Overcomplicating with equations

---
